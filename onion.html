Why Do We Cry While Cutting Onions?

Have you ever wondered why your eyes start watering the moment you begin cutting an onion? It may seem strange that a simple vegetable can make us cry, but there is actually some fascinating chemistry behind it.

What Happens Inside an Onion When We Cut It?

An onion is made up of millions of tiny cells. Inside these cells, different chemicals are stored separately. As long as the onion remains intact, these chemicals do not react with each other. However, when we cut, slice, or chop an onion, many of these cells are damaged.

The damaged cells release an enzyme called alliinase. This enzyme quickly reacts with sulfur-containing compounds naturally present in the onion. Through a series of chemical reactions, a volatile gas called syn-propanethial-S-oxide is produced. This gas easily spreads through the air and eventually reaches our eyes.

Why Do Our Eyes Produce Tears?

Our eyes are covered by a thin protective layer of moisture known as the tear film. When the onion gas comes into contact with this moisture, it dissolves and forms irritating substances. The sensitive nerves present on the surface of the eye detect this irritation and immediately send signals to the brain.

The brain responds by activating the tear glands, which begin producing extra tears. These tears are part of the eye's natural defense mechanism. Their purpose is not to express emotion but to wash away the irritating chemical and protect the delicate tissues of the eye.

How Can We Prevent Onion Tears?

Fortunately, there are several ways to reduce or prevent onion tears. Refrigerating the onion for about 30 minutes before cutting slows down the chemical reactions inside the onion. Using a very sharp knife causes less damage to the onion's cells. Cutting onions in a well-ventilated area, using a fan, or wearing protective goggles can also help.

Conclusion

The next time you find yourself crying while preparing onions, remember that it is not the onion making you emotional—it is simply chemistry in action. A chain of reactions inside the onion produces a gas that irritates your eyes, and your body's defense system responds by producing tears to keep your eyes safe and healthy.
